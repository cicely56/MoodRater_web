Welcome to the music "Mood Rater"

You are invited to take part in this research study on time-varying music emotion perception in live performance. This study is conducted by Simin Yang from Centre for Digital Music, Queen Mary University of London, as a part of her doctoral research. 

Procedures of the study:

  • Task 1:  You'll be asked to watch a video of a live classical music performance recording (17 minutes in length), and to rate the emotion you perceived from the music in the meantime using a two-dimension space: Valence (happy-sad continuum) and Arousal (excited-relaxed continuum) (More details in next page). You may pause the video player or play it back if needed. 
  • Task 2: You will be asked to review 9 short segments selected from the recording, as well as your emotion ratings within the segments, and then to write down the reason behind your rating. 
  • Task 3: You will be asked to complete one questionnaire about your music preference and experience in the end. 

Instructions:

  • Please fill in your ID at the bottom of this page before starting the experiment, and fill in the SAME ID on the questionnaire page.
  • Please use headphones at your comfortable listening level.
  • The whole task takes around 50 minutes and you may take breaks between tasks.
